Social Entrepreneurs

Building a social incubator from scratch

SOCIALAB

Background

During my time with Socialab in Latin America, I collaborated with CORFO, the Chilean government's innovation and entrepreneurship department, to develop a new public financing fund for early-stage social enterprises.

Project Overview

We leverage our expertise in social innovation and work with social entrepreneurs to deliver a comprehensive proposal for the fund. Our research and design process involved:

Research & Analysis

Literature and Tools Analysis: Analyzed various tools within and outside government.

Stakeholder Engagement: Conducted open-ended interviews and surveys with social and environmental entrepreneurs, government workers, officers, and other stakeholders.

Context Understanding: Researched to understand the context and identify missing pieces for the fund.

Solution

Initial Idea: Developed an initial idea based on research insights.

Co-Creation: Co-created, iterated, and refined the idea based on feedback.

Resource Adaptation: Adapted available governmental resources to implement the final instrument.

Impact

Impacted Thousands: Impacted thousands of people's lives and will continue to do so.

Benefited Entrepreneurs & Users: Benefited not only entrepreneurs but also the users of their solutions.

Supported Social Enterprises: Supported the creation of new social enterprises tackling global challenges and improving the quality of life for millions of Chileans.

Skills & Methods

Primary & Secondary Research | Interviews | Stakeholder Engagement | Co-Creation | Solution Development | Resource Adaptation

Outcome

The Ministry of Economy of Chile has utilized this instrument to support the creation of new social enterprises, demonstrating my ability to develop impactful solutions through design research and collaboration.
